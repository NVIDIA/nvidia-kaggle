When doing a quick EDA, I noticed there are a few type well files that are      
exactly the same but assigned to different horizontal wells. In some cases this 
makes sense (wells that are oriented adjacent or on top of each other), but in  
other cases the wells are quite distant from each other. Why would wells some   
distance apart be using exactly the same type well? Is this intentional?

Typically typewell selection is a manual process, the geologist picks the one   
that is close to the lateral and available at the time. Wells in the project    
were drilled over 10 years and when you were drilling certain lateral the       
closest typewell (the one the is close now) could not be available.             

Additionally the geologist may take TVT GR data from the nearby interpreted     
lateral to interpret the next lateral. Sometimes this helps a lot and this      
approach is called "pseudo-typewell". Some of the typewells in the project are  
actually "pseudo-typewells" - interpretations from nearby laterals drilled prior
to the current one.
